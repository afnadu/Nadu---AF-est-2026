None of this means starting over. It means a targeted cleanup + a content pivot. Here's exactly how.

### 1B. Following Reduction (The Most Urgent Fix)

**Target: 7,766 → under 600**

This is the biggest lever. A personal brand with 2K followers following 7.7K accounts looks like it's not worth following. The ratio should eventually invert.

**How to approach it (don't do it all in one day — Instagram flags mass unfollows):**
- Safe unfollow pace: ~150–200 accounts per day
- At 200/day: takes ~36 days to get to 600
- Use the built-in Instagram tool: Profile → Following → Sort by "Least Interacted With" (Instagram shows this filter natively)

**Unfollow in this order — highest volume, lowest value first:**

| Category | Estimated volume | How to identify |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Accounts that followed you back as part of follow-for-follow | High | Sort by "Least Interacted With" |
| Businesses and brands you don't actually engage with | High | Look for blue ticks, brand logos |
| Inactive accounts (no post in 6+ months) | High | Sort by "Earliest Following" |
| Meme, entertainment, viral accounts | Medium | Obvious from thumbnails |
| Acquaintances from past events you've lost touch with | Medium | Judgment call |
| Random travel/destination accounts followed mid-trip | Medium | Location-based accounts |

**Keep:**
- Close friends and family (real relationships)
- Accounts in your actual interest niche: founders, AI tools, startup culture, design, French culture
- Accounts whose posts you actually stop and read
- Local community: people in your actual city/industry scene
- Professional peers: other founders, investors, operators you know or want to know
- Accounts that signal your aesthetic and values (inspiration sources)

**Target "Keeps" list: 400–600 accounts**
This following list will itself become a signal — a curated list of 500 people tells a visitor exactly who you are.

### 1C. Grid Archive

You have 494 posts. You don't need to delete anything permanently — Instagram's Archive feature hides posts from your grid without deleting them. You can restore any post at any time.

**Goal:** Get your visible grid down to ~50 posts that collectively tell the story of who you are.

**What to KEEP visible (criteria):**
- Photos where your face is clear, warm, and on-brand (you looking confident, natural, genuine)
- Travel photos that show adventurousness and taste (Outback, Europe, NY are fine — they're part of your identity)
- Any photos at professional events, speaking, or with recognizable people in your industry
- Photos that show quality of life and values — not every sunset, but the ones that are genuinely beautiful
- Anything that could make a new visitor think "I want to know this person"

**What to ARCHIVE (criteria):**
- Group photos where you're barely visible or the context is unclear
- Low-quality or blurry shots that slipped through
- Very similar shots in a row (you probably have 3-4 sunset shots from the same trip — keep the best one)
- Photos from events where the context is completely private (weddings as a guest, etc. — great for Stories, not for the permanent grid)
- Anything that doesn't fit the visual direction you're moving toward

**Practical approach:**
1. Open your grid
2. Go post by post, asking: "Does this tell a visitor something good about who I am?"
3. If no → Archive
4. Target: your visible grid should be 40-60 posts, and the top 9 should be your best work

**After the archive, your grid should read:**
- Row 1-3: Your best current face shots, one travel, one professional context
- Overall vibe: warm, intentional, slightly adventurous, clearly a real person with a life AND a brain

## STEP 2 — Content System Going Forward

### The 4 Content Pillars (mapped to your grid)

Every post you make should fit one of these four buckets. This is how the account becomes coherent without losing your personality.

**Pillar 1 — BUILD (40% of posts)**
> What you're building at Nadu, how you're building it, what you're learning

Examples:
- "I've been testing 6 AI tools for [task]. Here's the honest ranking." (Reel or Carousel)
- "Week [X] of building Nadu. Here's what's working and what isn't." (Reel)
- "The decision I almost got wrong when starting Nadu." (Carousel)
- "I sent 50 cold emails last month. Here's what I learned." (Carousel)

This is your differentiation. Nobody else on Instagram is showing the Nadu story from the inside.

**Pillar 2 — FRAMEWORK (25% of posts)**
> Teachable frameworks, insights, and opinions from your experience

Examples:
- "5 things I know about building a brand in 2026 that I didn't know in 2024" (Carousel)
- "Hot take: most personal brands on Instagram are doing this wrong" (Reel)
- "How I actually structure my week as a solo founder" (Carousel)
- "The AI tool I use for [specific task] — and exactly how I use it" (Tutorial Reel)

**Pillar 3 — LIFE (25% of posts)**
> Personal moments that show your character, values, and world

Examples:
- Day-in-the-life Reel (real, unfiltered — morning routine of a founder)
- Travel content (but now framed with a perspective: "What building a company taught me about [place]")
- Cultural identity: French perspective on Australian business culture
- Books, music, what's inspiring you this month

The key shift: you already have this content. The upgrade is adding *your voice* to it — a caption or frame that ties it to who you are, not just what you did.

**Pillar 4 — TAKE (10% of posts)**
> Opinions, commentary, and conversation starters

Examples:
- "Unpopular opinion about [startup/AI/brand trend]"
- React to something happening in the founder world
- "Everyone is building with AI in 2026. Here's what most of them are missing."

### Caption Formula

Every caption should follow this structure:

```
[Hook — one sentence, front-loads the point or creates curiosity]

[2-3 sentences of value, story, or context]

[Question or CTA: "What's your take?" / "Save this." / "Send to a founder who needs this."]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3 #hashtag4 #hashtag5
```

**Hashtag targeting (3-5 only, 2026 standard):**
- 1 broad: `#founder` or `#entrepreneur`
- 2 medium: `#buildinpublic` `#aistartup` `#femalefounders`
- 1-2 niche: `#nadulife` (your own brand tag) + one hyper-specific per post topic

## STEP 3 — Visual Identity System

### Color Palette

Based on what I see in your grid — warm sunsets, natural tones, earthy travel — your aesthetic is already leaning toward a warm, natural palette. Lock it in:

**Primary palette:**
- Warm white / cream: `#F7F3EE` (backgrounds)
- Deep charcoal: `#1C1C1C` (text)
- Terracotta / burnt coral: `#C8614A` (accent — for Reel thumbnails, highlight covers)
- Warm sand: `#D4A574` (secondary accent)

This palette works because:
- It's warm and human (not cold tech-blue)
- It stands out in a feed of white/minimal personal brand accounts
- It connects naturally to your existing aesthetic (you already photograph warm)

### Reel Thumbnail Template

Create one template in Canva and use it for every Reel:
- Background: terracotta or cream
- 3-5 word title in bold sans-serif (white or charcoal)
- Your face in a circle OR a simple icon
- No clutter — just the title and one visual element

When visitors see your grid, all Reel thumbnails should be immediately recognizable as yours.

### Photo Style

For future personal photos:
- Shoot or select photos with consistent warm tones (golden hour light, indoor warm lighting)
- Avoid flat overhead white light — it doesn't match your grid
- Consistent "camera left" composition habit (face slightly to one side, not dead-center) gives a more editorial feel
