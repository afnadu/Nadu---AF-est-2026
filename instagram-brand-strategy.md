## 4. Content Pillars

Four pillars that unify all your interests without making the account feel scattered. Each has a clear audience payoff.

### Pillar 1 — THE BUILD (40% of content)
*Tech / AI / Startup process*

Your primary growth engine. This is your zone of differentiation.

**What this looks like:**
- "Here's the AI tool I used to cut my [task] time by 80%"
- "I launched [thing]. Here's what I got wrong."
- "Week 3 of building Nadu: what surprised me"
- Revenue/progress numbers shared honestly (even when small)
- Product decisions explained: "Why we chose X over Y"
- Behind-the-scenes of the actual work, not the polished result

**Why it works:** Build-in-public content is chronically underserved on Instagram vs. Twitter/X. There's a real audience gap for founders who share the messy middle, not just the highlight reel.

---

### Pillar 2 — THE FRAMEWORK (25% of content)
*Teachable insights from your experience*

Your authority builder. Carousels live here.

**What this looks like:**
- "5 things I wish I knew before starting a company"
- "How I use AI to do [specific task] as a solo founder"
- "The mistake most first-time founders make with their brand"
- Frameworks you've developed (even simple 2x2 matrices)
- "Hot takes" with actual reasoning behind them

**Why it works:** Carousels drive 2x the saves of Reels. Saves are the second-strongest algorithm signal in 2026. Educational carousels get shared to founders' group chats — a direct feeds-per-reach hit.

---

### Pillar 3 — THE LIFE (25% of content)
*Lifestyle + personality layer*

Your trust builder. Humanizes the brand.

**What this looks like:**
- Day-in-the-life as a founder (unfiltered)
- "What I actually ate / did / watched this week"
- Cultural identity (French-born perspective on work, ambition, aesthetics)
- Books, tools, music that shape how you think
- Honest reflections on energy, focus, burnout

**Why it works:** This is the content that makes people *feel* like they know you. It converts algorithm reach (from Pillar 1) into actual loyal followers. Without it, the account feels like a media company, not a person.

---

### Pillar 4 — THE TAKE (10% of content)
*Opinion / POV posts*

Your engagement driver. Comment-bait done with integrity.

**What this looks like:**
- "Unpopular opinion about [industry trend]"
- "Everyone's talking about X. Here's why I think they're missing the point."
- React to a news story or trend with your actual take
- "Hot or not: [specific tool / practice / trend]" — invites replies

**Why it works:** Comment velocity is a meaningful algorithm signal. Opinion posts generate real back-and-forth, which triggers Story replies and DMs — the strongest 2026 ranking signals.

## 5. Content Format Strategy

| Format | Role | Your Target Mix | Cadence |
|--------|------|----------------|---------|
| **Reels** | Discovery — new audience reach | 50% | 3x/week |
| **Carousels** | Authority — saves, shares, depth | 30% | 2x/week |
| **Stories** | Relationship — daily touchpoint | Daily | 3-5 frames/day |
| **Single image** | Culture / aesthetic moments | 10% | 1x/week |
| **Lives** | Community building | Optional | Monthly |

### Reel formula that works in 2026
- **Hook (0-2 sec):** Text on screen + spoken hook that creates a curiosity gap
- **Middle (3-25 sec):** The actual value — fast, no filler
- **CTA (last 3 sec):** "Save this" / "Comment [X] if you agree" / "Send this to a founder you know"
- **No intros.** Cut straight to the point.
- Captions: 2-3 sentences, keyword-rich, front-loaded

### Carousel formula
- Slide 1: Bold claim or question (the hook)
- Slides 2-6: One point per slide, no dense text
- Last slide: Summary + follow CTA + save prompt
- Always use "swipe →" language in caption to increase completion

## 7. Visual Identity & Grid Aesthetic

### Brand palette (recommendation)
Pick 2-3 core colors and stick to them across everything:

**Option A — Minimalist founder (clean, architectural)**
- Off-white / cream background (`#F5F1EB`)
- Warm black / charcoal text (`#1C1C1C`)
- One accent: burnt terracotta or deep sage

**Option B — Tech-meets-warmth (modern, approachable)**
- White backgrounds with soft warm tones
- Indigo or deep teal as accent (`#2D3A8C` or `#1A5C6B`)
- Gold/amber for highlights

### Grid rhythm (every 9 posts = one complete grid view)
Plan your next 9 posts as a visual set:
- Alternate between text-heavy carousels and visually led Reels/images
- Every third post: face / personal photo to break up the brand content
- Thumbnail consistency: same font, same color treatment on Reel covers

### Reel cover thumbnails
- Custom cover (not a random frame from the video)
- Same font, same color bar, consistent placement
- 3-5 word title on cover — these are your "mini headlines" in the grid

## 9. Algorithm Optimization — The 2026 Playbook

The Instagram algorithm in 2026 ranks primarily on:

1. **DM shares (sends per reach)** — highest weight. Design content explicitly to be forwarded.
2. **Saves** — second highest. Educational and reference content wins here.
3. **Watch time / completion rate** — especially for Reels. Hook in 2 seconds.
4. **Profile clicks** — content that makes people want to know more about you.
5. **Comments (quality)** — back-and-forth conversations, not one-word replies.

### Tactical implications:
- End every Reel with: *"Send this to a founder who needs to hear it"* — this directly targets the #1 signal
- End every carousel with: *"Save this for when you're building your [X]"* — targets signal #2
- Ask a question in every caption — drives comment chains
- Never buy engagement — fake interactions destroy your signal-to-noise ratio

### Story mechanics that drive reach:
- Use polls, question stickers, and sliders — Story replies count as strong engagement signals
- Reply to every DM personally for the first 6 months (when volumes are manageable)
- Stories with "share to feed" prompts when you post something new

## 10. Instagram SEO — Caption & Keyword Strategy

Instagram is now a search engine. 50%+ of new followers find accounts through search in 2026.

**Where to place keywords:**
1. Name field (most important — "Alexandra French · Founder & Builder")
2. Bio (include: founder, AI, startup, build in public)
3. Caption first sentence (front-load the topic)
4. On-screen text in Reels
5. Alt text on images (set manually in Advanced Settings)

**Caption structure:**
```
[First sentence with keyword] — the hook, the claim, the question.

[2-4 sentences of value or context]

[CTA: question / "save this" / "send to..."]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3 #hashtag4 #hashtag5
```

**Hashtag strategy (2026):**
- Use only 3-5 hashtags per post (volume no longer helps)
- Mix: 1 broad (#founder), 2 medium (#buildinpublic, #aistartup), 2 niche (#femalefounders, #naduco)
- Avoid overused tags (#entrepreneur has 100M+ posts — you won't surface)

## 11. The Underserved Angle — Your Differentiation Opportunity

**The gap:** Most Instagram "founder" content is either:
a) Polished success theater ("I went from 0 to $1M") — unrelatable
b) Pure inspirational fluff ("Believe in yourself") — forgettable

**What's underserved:** A female founder in tech/AI who documents the *real-time* process — the tool experiments, the product pivots, the messy thinking-out-loud — without either false modesty or false confidence.

**Your specific edge:**
- French cultural perspective on building a company (different POV from US-centric startup content)
- AI-native founder building in 2026 (timely, searchable, in-demand)
- The Nadu brand story as a live narrative (your audience follows a journey, not a persona)

**Content angles competitors aren't doing well:**
- "What I learned about [business topic] from growing up French"
- "I tested 10 AI tools for [founder task]. Here's the honest ranking."
- "The startup advice I got that was completely wrong for me"
- Behind-the-scenes of your actual thinking process (voice memos, whiteboard photos, messy notes)
